Photo Tips and Requirements for Custom Painted Pet Portraits

Photo Is Key

The photographs I receive and use as references for my artwork is the absolutely most significant part of the painting process. Since I work from photographs, the completed portrait depends on the quality of the photos you send me. Photo is key, so please provide with the best photos you have and try to send as many photos as possible.

If you would like to photograph your pet specifically for a portrait, you might find the information below very helpful.

Mobile Phone, System Camera or Old Paper-based Photos?

Mobile Phones and Tablets

Today, it's easier than ever to capture and share precious moments thanks to mobile phones and tablets that we carry everywhere.

While these devices let us snap photos on the go, their quality often falls short as a reference for portraits.

Though good lighting and proximity can help, an artist can easily spot the clarity difference between mobile photos and those from a high-quality digital camera.

Old Paper-based Photos

Many treasured memories are in old analog photos, though they often lack portrait quality.

Mail these to my studio for review; usable ones will be scanned in high resolution, and the original returned with the artwork.

If your pet is alive, consider taking new digital photos or ask for help from family or friends.

Positioning And Getting On Your Pets Level

Capturing the Best Angle

When photographing your pet, avoid looking down at them. Instead, capture their image from their eye level, as this perspective aligns with how the portrait will be viewed when hung on your wall.

For small animals, crouch down to their level or place them on a raised surface like a table. For larger pets, standing at their eye level is usually sufficient.

Perfecting the Shot

Visualize the final photo through the camera lens before clicking the shutter—if it looks good, take the shot!

Aim to capture your pet gazing slightly to the left or right of the camera, as this adds depth and prevents their face from appearing flat. Ensure both eyes are visible for a balanced and engaging portrait.

Natural Lighting

Whenever it is possible, try to take photos outside in natural lighting rather than inside with flash.

Even though it is often preferred to take photos on a sunny day, try to avoid photos with strong sunlight or too dark shadows which would make the pets colors fade away.

If you are unable to take photos outside, take photos indoors and place your pet close to a window without flash. Some natural daylight will

Fill The Entire Frame

For a full-body portrait, keep your pet close in the frame with a bit of background, ensuring they're the main focus. If your pet is too far, the details may not be sharp.

For a head and chest portrait, fill the frame with your pet's full head and neck without cropping any desired parts. Using a full-body shot to zoom in for detail won't work, as zooming often blurs the image.

I paint only what I see, so it's best to start with clear, close images.

Photo size

Try to email me your photos in original size, downloaded directly from your camera. If photos are reduced in size they are less clear, sharp and much important information is lost.

The amount of pixels can vary depending on which digital camera you were using, my own camera creates 5472 x 3648 pixel images.

I can accept any large size image though email or dropbox, so please send them at their original size.

If you don't have the original sized photo and your are unsure if I can use it as reference, me an email for review.

Be Patient and Have Fun

Capturing your pet's personality takes time, patience, and a relaxed environment.

Don't rush; use treats or toys to keep your pet comfortable, and carry a camera to catch those natural, joyful moments. A happy face is always the most beautiful!

Be creative, and look for spontaneous, “wow” moments in your pet's everyday life.

If your pet has passed and you only have older photos, feel free to email them for review; while a large oil painting may not be possible, other portrait options are available.
